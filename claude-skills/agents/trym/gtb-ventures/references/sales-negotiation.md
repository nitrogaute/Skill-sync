# Sales & Negotiation Frameworks

## Harvard Negotiation Tactics
*Source: "HARVARD Negotiation Tactics"*

### BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)
- Always know your backup option before negotiating
- Having alternatives = confidence + leverage
- Example: Knowing you have a $9k car option when negotiating for a $10k vehicle

### Psychological Techniques
- **Anchoring:** Set the tone with your first number. Even experienced negotiators are affected. List at $500 when willing to accept $300.
- **Contrast principle:** Show expensive options first → moderate prices seem like bargains
- **Silence:** Uncomfortable pauses → concessions and information leakage. Be comfortable with quiet.
- **Flinch:** Non-verbal surprise reaction → counterpart questions their own offer
- **Mirroring:** Repeat their words subtly → builds trust, encourages them to reveal more

### Value Creation
- Focus on underlying interests, not stated positions. Ask "why" behind demands.
- Expand the pie: bulk purchases + lower prices = mutual wins
- **Bundling:** Beyond single issues — add bonuses, flexibility, vacation days alongside salary
- **Nibble:** Small requests after major agreement is reached
- **Reciprocal concessions:** Offer value first → trigger obligation psychology
- **Fair standards:** Ground in objective criteria (market data) → harder to dispute

## Selling Without Being Salesy
*Source: "Selling Without Being Salesy" — Chris Do*

### Mindset Shift
- **Sales = change management.** Help clients move from current state → desired future state.
- Don't convince people to buy. Help them align goals with resources.
- Budget correlates with how significant the problem feels personally.

### The SALES Framework
- **S**erve — focus on the client, not yourself
- **A**sk — whoever asks more questions wins and controls the relationship
- **L**isten — shift from talking to listening
- **E**mpathize — understand their emotional state
- **S**ummarize — reflect back what you heard to confirm understanding

### Tactical Techniques
- **Pain point opener:** Identify top 3 problems clients typically face before they mention them
- **Price objection preempt:** "I'm most likely going to be the highest bidder on this"
- **Neutral observational questions:** Non-threatening, open-ended discovery
- **The $200K lesson:** At high price points, clients can hire anyone in the world. You must differentiate through approach, not just skills.
  - Chris Do: Changed approach → revenue doubled ($2.2M → $3.9M), close rate tripled (20% → 75%)

## Hormozi CLOSER Framework
*Source: "13 Years of No BS Business Advice" — Alex Hormozi*

- **C**larify why prospects engaged
- **L**abel their problems
- **O**verview past experiences (increases urgency)
- **S**ell the vacation (outcome), not the plane ride (process)
- **E**xplain away concerns
- **R**einforce the decision post-purchase

### Proof > Promises
- Recent, visual, high-volume testimonials that begin with customer pain points
- Best time to request testimonials: moment of greatest customer satisfaction
- Sales occur at moments of greatest customer pain or need
