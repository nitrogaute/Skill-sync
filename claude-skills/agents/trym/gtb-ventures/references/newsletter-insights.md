### Key Investment Frameworks (for GTB Ventures portfolio)

**1. Geopolitical Events & Markets (2 Mar 2026)**
- Israel/USA attacked Iran, killed Khamenei
- Historical data: markets recover within 12 months after war/crisis events
- Worst 1-month drop after crisis: -10% (Korea 1950)
- **Takeaway:** Hold the plan. Don't panic-sell. Volatility = buying opportunity for long-term holders.

**2. Top-Heavy Index Funds (23 Feb 2026)**
- Concentration in global index funds is NOT historically unusual (data back to 1926)
- Study: Buy-and-hold beat timing based on concentration by 2x+
- Largest holding: 7.3% of S&P500, 5.5% of MSCI World — far from Denmark's Novo Nordisk at 35%
- Large companies are MORE stable (diversified revenue, better capital access)
- **Takeaway:** Don't overthink concentration. Stay in global index funds.

**3. China Opportunity (16 Feb 2026)**
- MSCI China priced at ~12.8x vs ~25x for MSCI USA
- Leading in EVs (BYD, Xpeng), robotics, drones, solar, AI (DeepSeek)
- Risks: Taiwan invasion, state vs investor interests, dictator governance
- **Takeaway:** Small allocation via emerging market funds. Don't go all-in.

**4. Investor Psychology — You Are Your Own Worst Enemy (9 Feb 2026)**
- System for handling emotions:
  1. **Limit losses** — "playground" portion (5-10% of portfolio) for speculation
  2. **Avoid worst mistakes** — if you FEEL you must buy → don't. If you FEEL you must sell → buy more.
  3. **Do less** — 2-3 speculative investments per year max (Buffett: "20 investments in a lifetime")
  4. **Write down what you feel** before making changes — checklist against emotional traps
  5. **Signs of tops:** "this time is different", everyone's positive, media bullish
  6. **Signs of bottoms:** "it'll never recover", good news doesn't move price, media like a funeral
- **GTB relevance:** Apply same discipline to business investments. Don't chase shiny objects. Journal before big decisions.

**5. Currency Hedging (2 Feb 2026)**
- NOK/USD moved significantly (11.48 → 9.63, -16%)
- Timing currency hedging historically doesn't work — even Finansnerden's own 100k experiment proved it
- **Takeaway:** Don't currency-hedge for timing. Only as part of long-term plan. Always hedge bond funds.

**6. Best Global Index Funds (26 Jan 2026)**
- Start with one global index fund (e.g., KLP AksjeGlobal Indeks)
- Layer on: Europe fund, emerging markets fund, small-cap fund — based on conviction
- Active global funds: only if genuinely different from index (e.g., First Global Focus)
- 2% annual fee costs 41% of gains over time

### Finansnerden's Recurring Themes
- Indeksfond > aktive fond (data-backed, consistent message)
- Emotions are the biggest risk, not markets
- Systems and plans beat timing and speculation
- Simple portfolio: global index + optional tilts
- Costs compound — minimize fees

## Ali Abdaal — Entrepreneurship & Founder Mindset

### Business Scale Context
- Ali's business: ~15 team members, $2M+/year expenses
- Products: Part-Time YouTuber Academy, LifeOS course, Lifestyle Business Academy (LBA), SuperOS app
- Newsletter: LifeNotes (personal) + FreedomNotes (business-focused)

### Key Frameworks

**1. The Bezos Throttle — Release Ideas at the Right Rate (18 Jan 2026)**
- Jeff Wilke to Bezos: "You have enough ideas per minute to destroy Amazon"
- Every new idea creates backlog, distraction, and organizational whiplash
- Ali kept LBA secret from team for 6-9 months until validated
- Validation sequence: mentor conversations → personal noodling → signing up to competitors → test videos → $1k Challenge bootcamp → full launch
- **GTB relevance:** CRITICAL for Gaute. Don't throw ideas at Trym faster than we can execute. Validate quietly before committing resources. 1-3 active projects max.

**2. The One-Thing Quarterly Quest (18 Jan 2026)**
- "What am I already doing that this would compete with?" — ask before any new commitment
- Meaningful progress on max 1-3 things at a time
- Ambitious people are more likely to burn out from overeating than from starvation
- **GTB relevance:** Maps directly to our "9 opportunities to evaluate" problem. Pick ONE to validate deeply, not spray across all 9.

**3. Work as Play — The God of War Insight (11 Jan 2026)**
- Noticed he was optimizing a VIDEO GAME for efficiency — same pattern as work
- Reframe: "What if every goal is an arbitrary video game, played for the enjoyment of the journey?"
- When he switched to "play mode" building SuperOS, he was more creative AND productive
- Feel-Good Productivity thesis: lightness > seriousness → better output
- **GTB relevance:** Building GTB Ventures should be FUN. If it feels like a grind, we're doing it wrong. Approach opportunity evaluation with curiosity, not pressure.

**4. Systems > Willpower — The Midnight Scroll (2 Jan 2026)**
- Even Ali (a "productivity expert") fell back into doom-scrolling when systems degraded
- His app blocker stopped working → didn't fix it → "I can control myself" → failed
- Solution: app blocking 10pm-8am, phone charges in different room
- **GTB relevance:** Build systems, not willpower. Automated workflows, reminders, constraints. This is exactly why Trym exists.

**5. The Subtle Buzz of Dissatisfaction (15 Feb 2026)**
- Low-level background anxiety: "there are Problems to solve in the business"
- Not loud enough to notice, but colors mood and experience
- Meditation insight: observe it, smile at it, "thank you for looking out for me"
- "There are always more layers" to peel back
- **GTB relevance:** Gaute juggling CTO + VP + GTB + family = this anxiety is real. Acknowledge it, don't let it drive panic-decisions.

### Ali's Business Model Observations
- **Validation before building:** $1k Challenge bootcamp before full LBA
- **Content → Community → Course funnel:** YouTube videos → newsletter → paid academy
- **Lifestyle Business Academy:** recurring workshops, curriculum iteration, direct student work
- **Product diversification:** courses + app (SuperOS) + YouTube revenue
