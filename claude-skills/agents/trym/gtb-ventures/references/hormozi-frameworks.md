# Hormozi Frameworks

## Pricing Principles

Source: Alex Hormozi — Wealth Distribution & Tiered Pricing

- **Sell to wealthy customers.** Top 10% control 69% of all wealth. Serving one customer at 100k is easier than 1,000 at 100.
- **5-10x pricing tiers.** Each tier ~20% of customers qualify. Example: 800 @ $10, 200 @ $100, 40 @ $1,000, 8 @ $5,000+.
- **Close rate diagnostics:**
  - 60-80% close → underpriced 2-3x
  - 50-60% close → 1.5-2x room
  - 30-40% close → sweet spot
- **Value-to-cost ratio matters more than absolute price** for wealthy customers.
- Prefer B2B and premium niches over mass-market consumer plays.

## Quality & Talent

Source: Alex Hormozi — 13 Years of Business Lessons

- **Sell to wealthy first, then move downmarket.** Middle market kills businesses. Premium customers pay more, cost less to serve relative to expectations.
- **Talent is the constraint.** Every hire should raise the average. A+ players deliver 5-10x of B players. Test: can they explain exactly how they generate revenue?
- **Better beats bigger.** "100 golden BBs" — improve one thing per quarter per function. Quality compounds, growth creates bloat.
- **"The magic you're looking for is in the work you're avoiding."** Usually means: your core product isn't good enough yet.

## Customer Acquisition

Source: Alex Hormozi — Customer Acquisition Masterclass

- **Give away what competitors charge for** — make it better than their paid version. Creates massive lead flow.
- **Fewer, better people.** One elite marketer = 100x average. Tremendous leverage role.
- **Outspend via LTV, not lower CAC.** Ads only get more expensive. LTV should continuously increase.
- **Minimize "need to believes."** Fewer things customers must accept = faster conversion.
- **Affiliates:** Give 100% of a carved-out product piece, not 20% commission. Make partnerships irresistible.
- **Damaging admissions:** Acknowledge negatives upfront to amplify credibility on positives.

## Founder Mindset

Source: Alex Hormozi — Escaping Poverty & Building Wealth

- **Ownership first.** "It's my fault" = power to change. Blame = giving control away.
- **Focus is what you say NO to.** Hormozi had 9 failed businesses (6 simultaneous) before consolidating to the one that sold for $46M.
- **Ignorance debt:** The gap between what you earn and what you could earn if you knew how. Every dollar into skills compounds.
- **Volume before leverage.** When you lack resources, compensate with extreme work volume. 12x30 challenge: 12 hrs/day for 30 days.
- **One thing until it works.** Don't spread across multiple ventures. Obsessive concentration on one bet.
- **Success happens slower than expected, then faster than you can imagine** — but only if you don't stop.

## Business Model Selection

Source: Alex Hormozi — How To Grow ANY Business FASTER

Four fundamental shapes — know which you're building:

| Model | Growth | Bottleneck | Key Metric |
|-------|--------|-----------|------------|
| E-commerce | Stair-step | Inventory/supply chain | Brand vs. DR ratio (70/30) |
| Service | Slow & steady | Talent acquisition/training | Pricing power |
| Education | Spike → plateau | Retention (students graduate) | Split one-time vs. recurring |
| Software | Slowest start, infinite scale | Capital + retention | Revenue retention >100% |

- **Match your personality to the model.** Don't fight the shape.
- **Most problems are features of the model, not bugs.** Stop panicking about things inherent to your business type.
- **Service = teaching business.** Systemize delivery, narrow avatar, build recruiting/onboarding.
- **Brand is the ultimate moat** across all models.
- **Pricing power test:** If supply-constrained and good, raise prices consistently.

## Unit Economics & Scaling

Source: Alex Hormozi — Trash Collection Business Turnaround

- **One channel, one customer type.** Don't spread across 5 channels and 2 segments. Find the winner and go all-in.
- **Reverse-engineer the cash flow constraint.** Design offer structure so you collect enough cash DAY 1 to cover acquisition + fulfillment.
- **Key metrics to nail before scaling:** LTV:CAC ratio (target 20:1), monthly churn (<1%), lifetime gross profit per customer, upfront cash collected vs. cost-to-serve.
- **"If only this one thing were true, then what stops me?"** Strip complexity until one lever remains. Then pull it.
- **Scale with volume, not complexity.** 10 salespeople × 5 deals/day beats a clever funnel every time at early stage.

## Wealth Building & Skill Investment

Source: Alex Hormozi — Your Financial Goals Are Dangerously Wrong

- **Inflation destroys savings goals.** $1M at retirement = $170k purchasing power. Every dollar saved today = $13 at retirement. Every dollar spent = 80x lost future value.
- **Skills investment >> passive compounding.** $2k into skills generating $2k/mnd extra vastly outperforms 13x passive return.
- **Mandatory learning budget.** 1-10% of income. Free (YouTube) → $10-200/mnd (programs) → $5k-35k (coaching).
- **Give value first in premium communities.** 6 hours helping others → 1 hour of expert time. Best ROI learning tactic.
- **"I will pull cash from my future forward."** Invest in revenue-generating skills now, not save for decades.
- **Compress the timeline.** Starting at 18 vs 28 = 80x vs 33x returns. Early dollars are exponentially more valuable.

## Value Equation & Offers

Source: Alex Hormozi — $100M Offers (via Ali Abdaal)

- **Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time + Effort + Sacrifice)**
- Increase value by: bigger dream outcome, higher perceived likelihood, less time/effort/sacrifice required.
- **Sell what the market wants, not your hobby.** Pivoting from "teach public speaking to immigrants" to "speed up accounting firm client onboarding" = boring but highly valuable.
- 10 × $10k is far easier than 10,000 × $10, especially without an audience.

## Comprehensive Business Playbook (79-min Masterclass)

Source: "13 Years of No BS Business Advice" — Alex Hormozi (full video)

### Pricing Psychology
- **Anchor high:** Start with 10-100x more expensive offer you never plan to sell. Makes real offer look affordable.
- **10% of customers want the most expensive thing.** Missing whales = missing revenue.
- **Raise prices regularly.** $100 in 2017 = $121 today just to maintain purchasing power. Most businesses haven't raised in years.
- **Test dramatic increases.** Doubling price with only 35% conversion drop = 2x absolute profit (higher margins per customer).

### Customer Acquisition Rules
- **Obscurity is the #1 threat.** Use the "4x4 rule": 4 hours daily on core four: reach out to known contacts, contact strangers, create content, run ads.
- **Rule of 100:** 100 minutes of content, OR 100 outreaches, OR $100/day on ads. Minimum baseline.
- **Under $100k/mo revenue → all focus on advertising.** Get customer volume to iterate product.
- **After $100k/mo → fix delivery/retention** before scaling further. Don't acquire and lose.

### Focus & Scaling
- **Under $1M/year: ONE channel, ONE avatar, ONE product.** No exceptions. Multiple approaches = dilution.
- **Hormozi didn't open a second channel until $4M/month.**
- **Start free → build proof.** Free services → testimonials → case studies → paid offers. Works at every level.

### CLOSER Sales Framework
- **C**larify → **L**abel → **O**verview → **S**ell the vacation → **E**xplain away concerns → **R**einforce post-purchase
- Proof > promises. Recent, visual, high-volume testimonials starting with customer pain points.
- Request testimonials at peak satisfaction. Sell at peak pain.

## Creative Scaling & Sales Infrastructure

Source: Alex Hormozi — Scaling $6.4M Travel Rewards Biz (Joel/Joot)

### Kaleidoscope Method (Creative Multiplication)
- Take ONE winning ad creative and produce 20-30 variations:
  - AI filters, black & white, sepia, cartoonized, 3-sec animated video from stills
- **Copy can run for months (3-6 months); creative must refresh constantly.**
- Diversify avatars/people in ads — Facebook algorithm targets based on who APPEARS in the ad, so different faces = different audiences automatically.
- Test organically first, then convert winners to paid with 5-second CTA appended.

### UGC Loops (User-Generated Content)
- Incentivize customers to post 60-second trip/result montages on social platforms.
- Exchange: exclusive training materials, checklists, or perks for UGC.
- **iPhone selfie-style > polished professional content.** Authenticity wins.
- Hire a "hyper caffeinated 20-year-old" native to TikTok/IG for trending meme-style content.
- Visual businesses (travel, fitness, food, real estate) = natural UGC goldmine.

### Damaging Admissions (Sales Technique — expanded)
- **Frontload ALL limitations before the pitch.**
- "If you want X, this isn't for you" — list every trade-off upfront.
- Lets prospects self-qualify rather than building objections during presentation.
- Works because: prospect already WANTS the benefit but doesn't BELIEVE it. Transparency builds credibility.
- Example: "You save 70-90% on travel, but you need flexibility on dates. If you can only travel on specific days, this won't work."

### Parallel Dialer Sales Scaling
- Each rep calls 10 numbers simultaneously via parallel dialer technology.
- Auto-connects answered calls to available reps. Crucial at ~5% pickup rate.
- **Lead scoring at opt-in:** Collect qualification data via "free assessment" offer.
- Identify the 2 critical qualification factors for your business and filter aggressively.

### Key Metrics Reality Check
- **LTV:CAC of 1.4:1 is dangerously thin.** Target 3:1 minimum.
- Creative quality is almost always the constraint, not ad budget.
- If spending more doesn't get proportionally more results → creative is saturated, not audience.
- Breaking through ad spend ceilings: fix creative diversity first, then scale budget.

## The $100K Breakthrough — 6-Step Roadmap

Source: Alex Hormozi — https://youtu.be/jfW6gL6hKhk

The first $100K in the bank is the inflection point — eliminates survival-mode thinking, enables long-term decisions.

### Step 1: Cut All Costs
- Eliminate every non-essential expense. Cheapest housing, no eating out, wear existing clothes.
- Creates cash flow for reinvestment in skills and opportunities.

### Step 2: Save Time (444 Split)
- Self-employed: 4h promote, 4h deliver, 4h build future.
- Maker vs manager: makers need uninterrupted deep-work blocks.
- **"First 4-6 hours of the day to yourself"** — the #1 productivity habit.

### Step 3: Research Skills People Already Pay For
- B2B: examine business functions (ads, content, funnels).
- B2C: check your own credit card statements.
- **111 Rule:** 1 product, 1 customer type, 1 channel → until $1M.

### Step 4: Learn Through 10,000 Iterations
- Change behavior, not just consume content.
- Study top 10% of outcomes, identify what differs from bottom 90%, test systematically.
- Fastest method: 1-on-1 coaching from someone excellent.

### Step 5: Spend on Tools, Implementation, Attempts
- Three investment areas: tools/software, courses/communities, trial attempts (ad spend, equipment).
- Don't rebuild from scratch when affordable solutions exist.

### Step 6: Never Increase Lifestyle
- Bank the difference. Hormozi paid $400 rent while earning $20K/month.
- Everything beyond food+shelter = profit for reinvestment.
