# Priestley & Ducker — Lifestyle Business Frameworks

## Zero to $1M Roadmap

Source: Daniel Priestley (via Ali Abdaal)

- **Idea ranking:** Score ideas on Pain (severity), Prize (dream outcome), Payment (willingness + ability to pay).
- **Validate with 30 conversations.** 30 samples = initial validation. 150 = fine-tuning. Your best idea captures only ~40% of what customers actually want pre-launch.
- **Team stages:** Scout team (2 people) → Squad (4, including associate KPI — someone lending name/reputation) → Go-to-market (8) → Scale-up (30).
- **1:1 sales are mandatory** even for low-priced products. Customer conversations reveal what you can't guess. Priestley did 30 1:1 sales meetings for a new software venture despite being a billionaire.
- **Conversion math:** Cold traffic = 1/200 (0.5%). Qualified leads = 1/50 to 1/80. Work backward from revenue target.
- **Perfect Repeatable Week:** Structure consistent weekly activities — team meetings, sales calls, customer feedback, marketing. Example: 3 × $5k/week = $15k revenue needs ~600 leads/week at 1/200.
- **Product-for-Prospects (P4P):** Low-commitment entry (webinars, scorecards, free trials) → funnel into core product. Enables 1-to-many selling.
- **Value creation loop:** Founder-opportunity fit → MVP → Product-market fit → Go-to-market → Scale-up → Exit.
- **Lifestyle business = stop at go-to-market** (8 people, semi-exit). Avoids the intense scale-up grind.
- **Relatability > expertise.** Customers prefer learning from someone a few steps ahead, not billionaires. Being relatable is a competitive advantage.

## Entrepreneurship Fundamentals

Source: Daniel Priestley (via Ali Abdaal) — Build a Business From Scratch

- **Shot Caller model.** Founders do step 1 (vision) and step 10 (commercialize). Steps 2-9 outsourced to labor/AI. If you want "business in a box" you want a job.
- **766 Apprenticeship.** 6 months with a founder doing 7-fig revenue, 6-fig profit. Not for cash — for experience, self-awareness, commercial awareness, network.
- **Asymmetric risk.** Small downside (few thousand £), massive upside. The "90% fail" stat is a myth.
- **Pricing pyramid:** 1/100 has 15% of budget (£15k), 9/100 has 45% (£5k each), 90/100 share 40% (avg £445). Focus on the 10, not the 90.
- **First business sweet spot:** £1k-£10k price, 10-100 customers. Service/agency model.
- **Founder-opportunity fit > brilliant idea.** Origin story + vision + mission. Pret (sandwiches) > demolishing London (trillion-dollar idea).
- **Lifestyle business team:** 3-12 people = self-managing and fun. >13 = departments, silos, meetings about meetings.
- **Digital economy is the new land.** Those in digital see life improving yearly. Traditional employment gets harder. The gap widens.

## Lifestyle Business Design

Source: Chris Ducker (via Ali Abdaal) — 14-Lesson Framework

- **Speed limit, not speed target.** Just because you CAN work 15-hour days doesn't mean you should. Design the business around the life you want.
- **Target vs Market.** "Men in their 40s recovering from surgery" > "anyone who wants to lose weight." Specificity makes marketing easier and clients stickier.
- **Solve ONE problem deeply.** Ducker's Virtual Staff Finder: one problem (find VAs in Philippines), 9,000 clients, 15 years, acquired. Narrow > wide.
- **Reframe for premium.** Landscape photography course $300 → Retreat for burned-out corporate professionals $10,000. Same skill, different customer, 33x price.
- **Three currencies:** Time, energy, focus. Energy is most valuable — protect it with nutrition, sleep, movement.
- **Start as side project.** Don't quit the job. Ducker launched a magazine while working full-time in publishing.
- **Stop, Stay, Start audit.** Regularly evaluate: what should I stop doing? What's working (keep)? What new thing should I start?
- **"The main thing is the main thing."** One project to completion beats spreading across 5 half-finished ones.
- **Rolodex compounds.** Relationships become more valuable over time. Good people hang with good people. Invest in network early.
- **Consistency > quick wins.** Ducker now works Mon-Thu 10am-3pm, Fridays off. Less money than ever, happier than ever.
- **Lifestyle business ceiling:** Six to low seven figures profit. Not billionaire money, but full freedom + fulfillment.
