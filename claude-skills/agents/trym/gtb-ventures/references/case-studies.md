# Case Studies

## FireCut ($0 → $1M ARR in 12 months)

Source: Sahel (via Ali Abdaal) — AI video editing plugin for Premiere Pro

- **Playbook:** 50+ problem list → Hormozi filter → casual convo revealed pain point → hacky prototype in 2 days → 6-week beta with 20 users → launch
- **Hormozi filter applied:** Painful (editors spend 10-20hrs on repetitive tasks) + purchasing power (creators/businesses) + easy to target (YouTube/IG) + growing market (creator economy)
- **Growth engine:** 50-100 cold emails/day to creators. Free license + affiliate commission. VA in Pakistan ($300-500/mnd) runs it after 6 months. 10,000+ emails/year.
- **Conversion:** 30% reply → 15% positive → 5-10% make video about tool
- **Revenue curve:** $0 → $10k MRR (month 4) → $30k (month 8) → $90k (month 24). Linear $5k MRR growth/month.
- **Team:** 5 people (2 devs, 2 growth, founder). Software = 90%+ margin.
- **Price:** $24/mnd — almost arbitrary, based on competitor research.
- **Key lesson:** The hacky prototype was enough. First dev hire was "shocked the codebase worked at all." Ship fast, fix later.
- **Applicable to GTB:** Cold outreach machine is systematizable by Trym. Problem-first approach. SaaS margins. Niche plugin strategy.

## Portfolio Strategy (App Economy)

Source: Max (iOS dev) — 28 Apps in 8 Months

- **Portfolio > perfection.** Ship fast, let the market pick winners. 80/20: 4 of 28 apps generate bulk of $10k/mo revenue.
- **Validate before building:** Keyword research (Astro, 20%+ popularity, 60-70% difficulty), check competitor revenue (€100-200/mo minimum).
- **Reusable components = speed.** 90% shared codebase across apps. Onboarding, paywalls, settings = drag-and-drop.
- **Ship one feature, bug-free.** Don't polish. Don't add «one more killer feature». Ship, let users tell you, build next app.
- **Double down on winners.** After initial App Store boost fades, watch which stabilize/grow. Polish those, run ads.
- **Lean costs:** ~$290/mo (Cursor, OpenAI, Firebase, Astro) on $10k/mo revenue.
- **Applicable beyond apps:** Same portfolio-and-iterate logic works for micro-SaaS, tools, templates, any digital product.

## Prometora — Solo Niche SaaS ($0 → $850 MRR in 4 months)

Source: Rasmus (kode24, March 2026) — AI-powered marketplace builder, Norway

- **Product:** No-code marketplace builder (think Etsy/Airbnb/Finn). Stripe Connect, split payments, seller onboarding, reviews, dashboards — deep domain complexity.
- **Stack:** Claude + Next.js + Vercel AI SDK. One person builds and runs everything.
- **AI architecture:** 22 specialized tools (not one big prompt). AI *selects and executes*, doesn't generate. 14 visual editing tools + 8 component tools. Template-first: 100ms vs 3-5 sec.
- **Model routing:** Haiku for style changes (cheap/fast), Sonnet for planning/vision. "You don't need Opus to change a font size."
- **Pricing:** $39-199/mnd + 1-2% transaction fee. ARPU $88. Target: ~200 customers for $17k MRR.
- **Lock-in:** Natural — customers build up sellers, payment flows, shipping rules. High switching cost from value, not coercion.
- **Sales:** Zero ads. YouTube (@rasmus1000) + SEO. "One video/week, one page/week. After a year: 50 videos + 50 indexed pages. A moat no ad budget can buy."
- **Key insight:** "Niche beats general. Lovable is worth billions with hundreds of employees. I have zero employees and $850 MRR. But I solve a problem they'll never prioritize."
- **Applicable to GTB:** Perfect example of path #1 (high-ticket B2B niche). Domain expertise + AI as engine + content moat. Solo operator with SaaS margins. Proves one person can serve many customers when AI handles support, content, docs, and dev.

## Failed AI Business Models to Avoid

Source: "Unique AI business model"

- **AI automation agencies:** Constant custom builds, high churn. Owners want to quit/pivot.
- **AI content creation:** Unreliable ad revenue, platform crackdowns on AI content.
- **Freelance AI dev:** Competing with $10-20/mo AI coding tools.
- **AI dropshipping:** High capital, competing with Temu.
- **AI day trading bots:** 97% of persistent traders lose money.
