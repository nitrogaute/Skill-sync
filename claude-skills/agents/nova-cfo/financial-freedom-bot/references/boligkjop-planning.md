# House Purchase Planning

Reference for property purchase decisions. Target: 10-12M NOK house in the Stavanger/Sandnes area.

## Norwegian Mortgage Rules (Utlansforskriften)

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Max loan-to-value | 85% (15% equity required) |
| Max debt-to-income | 5x gross annual household income |
| Stress test | Must afford payments at current rate + 3 percentage points |
| Applies to | All debt combined (boliglan, studielan, billan, credit card debt) |

**For a 10-12M property:**

```
Equity required (15%):     1.5M - 1.8M NOK
Max loan at 5x on 1.5M income: 7.5M (single) — need dual income or more equity
Dokumentavgift (2.5%):     250K - 300K NOK
Total cash needed:         1.75M - 2.1M NOK
```

**Key constraint**: At 1.5M single income, max loan is 7.5M. For 10M+ property, need either:
- Dual income (Elsa's income adds to qualification)
- More equity (>15%)
- Combination of both

## FI Impact Analysis

House purchase has complex FI implications:

**Positive:**
- Mortgage principal payments = forced savings (builds equity)
- Property appreciation (historically 3-5% nominal in Stavanger)
- Stability and lifestyle value
- Bolig is 75% exempt from formuesskatt (25% verdsetting up to 10M)

**Negative:**
- Massive capital tied up in illiquid asset
- Opportunity cost: equity in house vs invested in market (historically higher returns)
- Increases monthly fixed costs, reducing savings rate
- Interest payments are a pure cost (though 22% tax deductible)

**Model both scenarios:**
1. Buy: equity in house + reduced monthly investing
2. Don't buy: continue renting + invest the would-be equity + invest the monthly savings difference

Compare net worth at 5/10/25 year horizons to determine which path reaches FI faster.
