# Pension Strategy Optimization

## Your Situation
- **Age:** 35 | **Employer:** Kongsberg Maritime | **Income:** ~1.5M NOK/year
- **AFP eligibility:** Yes (through KM) | **IPS:** 15,000 NOK/year | **Goal:** Financial freedom

## The Four Pension Pillars

### 1. Folkepensjon (State Pension)
- Full rights after 40 years of residence/work
- At 67 (after tax): ~220,000-245,000 NOK/year
- At 62 (reduced, after tax): ~170,000-185,000 NOK/year

### 2. AFP (Avtalefestet Pensjon)
- **Eligibility:** Must be in AFP-covered employment for 7 of last 9 years before 62
- At 62 (after tax): ~48,000-64,000 NOK/year (lifelong supplement)
- At 67 (after tax): ~64,000-80,000 NOK/year
- **Critical:** If you leave KM before age ~53, you risk losing AFP

### 3. OTP (Obligatorisk Tjenestepensjon)
- Employer contributes 5-7% of salary (1G to 12G)
- Accumulation at 62 (at 4% real return): 5.3-6.7M NOK
- After-tax income (drawn over 15 years from 62): ~290,000-310,000 NOK/year
- **Action:** Check fund allocation -- switch to 100% global equity index

### 4. IPS (Individuell Pensjonssparing)
- **2026 max:** 25,000 NOK/year (you contribute 15,000)
- Tax deduction: 22% = **5,500 NOK/year** at max
- Formuesskatt: 100% exempt
- Accumulation at 62 (25k/year, 4% real): ~1,250,000 NOK
- **It's an interest-free loan from the state -- always max it**

## How Pension Fits Into Financial Freedom

### Standard FI Number
Annual expenses 700k / 3.5% SWR = **20,000,000 NOK**

### Pension-Adjusted FI Number
- At 62, pension covers ~573-634k of 700k expenses
- Gap: only 66-127k/year
- Adjusted FI Number at 62: **~3,600,000 NOK**

### The Bridge Strategy
- **Before 62:** Need full portfolio to cover expenses
- **After 62:** Pension covers most/all expenses
- **Retire at 50?** Need ~10-12M portfolio to bridge ages 50-62, then pension takes over
- Much more achievable than the naive 20M FI number
