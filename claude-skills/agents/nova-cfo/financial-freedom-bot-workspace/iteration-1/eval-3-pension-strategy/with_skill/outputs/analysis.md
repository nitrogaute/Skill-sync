### Stage 3: Scenario Modeling

#### Your Four Pension Pillars

**Pillar 1: Folkepensjon (State Pension)**
- **Eligibility:** Full rights after 40 years of residence/work. You are 35 with ~10-13 years of earnings history -- on track for full rights by age 62-67.
- **Amount at 67 (after tax, ~25-30% effective pension tax rate):** ~175-210k kr/year
- **At 62 (early claiming, actuarial reduction ~20-25%, after tax):** ~130-160k kr/year
- **At 70 (delayed claiming, actuarial increase ~15-20%, after tax):** ~200-250k kr/year

**Pillar 2: AFP (Avtalefestet Pensjon)**
- **Eligibility requirements:**
  - Must be employed in AFP-covered company (KM qualifies) at age 62
  - Must have been in AFP-covered employment for **7 of the last 9 years** before turning 62
  - Must have pensjonsbeholdning above minimum threshold
  - Cannot receive disability pension (uforetrygd)
- **Amount at 62 (after tax):** ~60-90k kr/year as a lifelong supplement
- **Key feature:** AFP is a lifelong supplement -- it does NOT reduce if you also take folkepensjon. It adds on top.

**Pillar 3: OTP (Obligatorisk Tjenestepensjon) from KM**
- **Estimated contribution rate:** 5-7% of salary between 1G and 12G
- **Your salary (1.5M):** Contribution base = 1,500,000 - 124,028 (1G) = 1,375,972 kr. At 5%: ~68,800 kr/year. At 7%: ~96,300 kr/year.
- **Accumulation to age 62 (27 years at 5% real return):**
  - At 5% employer rate: ~3.7M NOK
  - At 7% employer rate: ~5.2M NOK
- **Drawdown over 10 years (62-72), after tax (~30-35%):** ~240-360k kr/year
- **Drawdown over 15 years (62-77), after tax:** ~170-240k kr/year

**Pillar 4: IPS (Individuell Pensjonssparing)**
- **Current contribution:** 15,000 kr/year
- **2026 maximum:** 25,000 kr/year
- **Tax deduction:** 22% of contribution = 3,300 kr/year (at 15k) vs **5,500 kr/year (at 25k)**
- **Formuesskatt exemption:** IPS balance is fully exempt from wealth tax. At 1% formuesskatt rate, a 1M IPS balance saves 10,000 kr/year in wealth tax.
- **Accumulation to age 62 (27 years at 5% real return):**
  - At 15k/year: ~810k NOK
  - At 25k/year: **~1,350k NOK** (1.35M)
- **Drawdown over 10 years (62-72), after pension tax (~25-30%):**
  - At 15k contribution: ~57-61k kr/year after tax
  - At 25k contribution: **~95-101k kr/year after tax**
- **Net benefit of increasing from 15k to 25k/year:**
  - Extra tax deduction: 2,200 kr/year (immediate)
  - Extra formuesskatt savings: grows with balance (long-term)
  - Extra pension income: ~38-40k kr/year more during drawdown
  - Total extra cost: 10,000 kr/year (but 2,200 comes back as tax deduction, so net cost ~7,800 kr/year)

## Key Insight: Pension as FI Accelerator

The most important takeaway is how pension transforms your FI math:

- **Before 62:** You need the full FI portfolio (~17M at 600k expenses). Pension cannot help.
- **At 62:** Pension income covers most or all of your lifestyle expenses. Required portfolio drops to ~0-2M.
- **Strategy implication:** You do not necessarily need 17M in investable assets. If you can build a "bridge portfolio" to cover ages 50-62 (roughly 7-8M in today's kroner), pension takes over from there.

This two-phase approach -- **bridge to 62, then pension-funded abundance** -- is far more achievable than building the full 17M FI portfolio.

**The AFP eligibility constraint is your biggest strategic risk.** If you leave AFP-covered employment to pursue GTB Ventures full-time before age ~55, you lose an estimated 60-90k kr/year in lifetime pension income. That is equivalent to having ~1.7-2.6M less in your FI portfolio. Factor this into any decision to leave KM.
