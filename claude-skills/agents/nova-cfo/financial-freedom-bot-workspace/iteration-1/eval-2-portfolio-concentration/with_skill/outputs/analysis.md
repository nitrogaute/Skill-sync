#### Tax Cost of Diversification Inside ASK

**This is the critical tax-efficiency question.** The answer is very favorable:

1. **Selling KOG inside ASK and buying index funds inside the same ASK = zero tax event.** ASK (aksjesparekonto) allows tax-free switching between eligible securities (listed shares and equity funds) within the account. You only pay tax when you withdraw cash from the ASK above your total historical deposits.

2. **Your unrealized gain on KOG is ~1.13M NOK** (cost basis ~380k on the 1.51M position, based on ~300% return). If you sold outside ASK, you would owe 37.84% * 1.13M = **~427,000 NOK in tax**. Inside ASK, this tax is **zero** as long as you reinvest.

3. **The only tax trigger is cash withdrawal from ASK.** As long as the freed-up capital is redeployed into index funds or other listed securities within the ASK, no tax is due.

4. **Formuesskatt consideration:** Listed shares in ASK get 20% verdsettelsesrabatt (taxed at 80% of market value). Index funds in ASK get the same treatment. So switching from KOG to index funds has **no formuesskatt impact** -- the treatment is identical.

**Bottom line: ASK makes this diversification essentially free from a tax perspective.** This is one of the most tax-efficient rebalancing opportunities in the Norwegian system.

#### What Concentration Risk Actually Means for Financial Freedom

Concentration risk is not about expected returns -- it is about the **variance of outcomes** and the **probability of catastrophic loss**.

Consider what happens if Kongsberg (or the defence sector broadly) faces a severe negative event:

1. **KOG/KM stock drops 40-50%** -- your portfolio loses 500-700k NOK
2. **Your KM salary is at risk** -- layoffs, restructuring, reduced bonus
3. **Your OTP pension fund** (likely invested partly in employer stock) is also hit
4. **Your employee share program** loses value simultaneously
5. **Your career market value** drops if the sector contracts

All five of these hit **at the same time.** This is the definition of correlated risk. A diversified portfolio breaks this correlation: even if KM/KOG drops 50%, your index fund exposure to US tech, healthcare, and global consumer companies is unaffected.

**In FI terms:** A 50% drawdown on a concentrated portfolio at your current stage could set you back 5-10 years on the FI timeline. The same event with a diversified portfolio costs 2-3 years. Over a 15-20 year FI horizon, this difference is enormous.

The mathematical intuition: **geometric returns (what actually compounds your wealth) penalize volatility.** A stock that goes +50% then -50% does not return to even -- it loses 25%. By reducing concentration, you reduce the volatility drag on your long-term compounding, even if the arithmetic expected return is slightly lower.

## Appendix: Why ASK Is Your Best Friend Here

The aksjesparekonto is specifically designed for this type of tax-efficient rebalancing:

| Action | Tax inside ASK | Tax outside ASK |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Sell KOG with ~1.13M unrealized gain | **0 NOK** | ~427,000 NOK (37.84%) |
| Buy global index fund with proceeds | **0 NOK** | 0 NOK |
| Receive dividends (reinvested) | **0 NOK** (until withdrawal) | 37.84% on each dividend |
| Formuesskatt treatment | 80% of market value (20% rabatt) | Same for listed shares |
| Withdraw cash above deposits | 37.84% on gain portion | N/A (already taxed) |

The only scenario where ASK is less favorable is if you need the cash for consumption (e.g., house down payment). In that case, you pay 37.84% on the gain portion of any withdrawal. But for rebalancing within the portfolio, ASK is a zero-tax wrapper.
