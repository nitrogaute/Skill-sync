# Burning Platform

## When to Use

Best for change management presentations, organizational transformation pitches, and any situation where you need to create urgency for action. Use when the audience is comfortable with the status quo and needs to be moved to act. Common in internal leadership presentations, strategy pivots, and "why we must change" narratives.

## Structure

### 1. The Platform is Burning (3–4 slides)
Show why the current state is unsustainable. Use data, trends, competitive pressure, regulatory changes, or market shifts to make the case that staying put is not an option. The emotional core: "The building is on fire — we cannot stay here."

**Purpose**: Destroy complacency. Make inaction feel more risky than action.

### 2. The Leap We Must Make (3–4 slides)
Describe the transformation required — what must change, what the new state looks like, and what it will take to get there. Be specific about scope, resources, and timeline. This is the bridge between fear and hope.

**Purpose**: Channel the urgency into a concrete direction. Show you have a plan, not just a warning.

### 3. The New Ground (2–3 slides)
Paint a picture of what success looks like after the transformation. Quantify the upside. Show early wins or proof of concept. Make the destination attractive enough to justify the difficulty of getting there.

**Purpose**: Provide hope and motivation. Balance the urgency of section 1 with a compelling vision.

## Slide Skeleton

| Slide | Section | Purpose | Key Question It Answers |
|-------|---------|---------|------------------------|
| 1 | Title | Set expectations | What is this about? |
| 2 | Burning | The uncomfortable truth | What's happening around us? |
| 3 | Burning | Data/evidence | How bad is it really? |
| 4 | Burning | What happens if we don't act | Where does inaction lead? |
| 5 | Burning | Why now, not later | Why can't we wait? |
| 6 | Leap | The transformation required | What must change? |
| 7 | Leap | The roadmap | How do we get there? |
| 8 | Leap | What we need (resources/decisions) | What does this require? |
| 9 | Leap | Early wins / proof | Is this even possible? |
| 10 | New Ground | Vision of success | What does the future look like? |
| 11 | New Ground | Quantified upside | What do we gain? |
| 12 | Ask | Commitment/decision | Are we doing this? |
| 13 | Q&A | Discussion | Open floor |

## Example Transitions

- Opening → Burning: "Let me start with something uncomfortable..."
- Burning → Leap: "So the question isn't whether we change — it's how fast we can move..."
- Leap → New Ground: "If we execute this, here's what we're building toward..."
- New Ground → Ask: "To get there, I need your commitment on three things today..."

## Pitfalls to Avoid

- **All fear, no hope**: If you spend 80% of the deck on the burning platform and 20% on the solution, you'll demoralize the audience instead of mobilizing them. Balance urgency with vision.
- **Exaggerating the threat**: If the data doesn't support "existential crisis", don't frame it that way. Audiences sense manipulation and it destroys trust. Be honest about the severity.
- **Vague transformation plan**: "We need to be more agile" is not a plan. Specifics — timeline, milestones, resource asks, accountability — are what make the Leap credible.
- **Ignoring the cost of change**: Acknowledge that the transformation will be hard. Audiences respect honesty about difficulty more than false optimism.
- **No early proof**: If possible, show that the transformation has already started working somewhere (pilot, prototype, comparable case). Pure theory is unconvincing.
