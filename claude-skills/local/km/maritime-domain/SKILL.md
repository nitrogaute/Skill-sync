# Maritime Domain Skill

This skill provides comprehensive domain knowledge for developing business cases in the maritime industry. It covers vessel types, market dynamics, regulatory frameworks, customer segments, and strategic considerations specific to the maritime sector.

## Overview

The maritime industry encompasses shipping, offshore operations, port services, and naval activities. It is a capital-intensive sector with long asset lifecycles (typically 20-30 years for vessels), cyclical market dynamics, and significant regulatory oversight. Business cases in this domain must account for the unique operational requirements, safety considerations, and commercial structures that define maritime operations.

Key characteristics of the maritime market:
- Global in nature with regional regulatory variations
- Highly fragmented ownership structures
- Strong emphasis on safety and environmental compliance
- Long procurement cycles and relationship-based sales
- Increasing digitalization and decarbonization pressures

## Vessel Types

Understanding vessel categories is essential for targeting solutions and sizing markets appropriately.

### Offshore Support Vessels

- **SOV (Service Operation Vessel)**: Purpose-built vessels for offshore wind farm maintenance. SOVs feature motion-compensated gangways for safe crew transfer, accommodation for 60-120 technicians, and warehousing capabilities. Day rates range from $25,000-$50,000 depending on specifications and market conditions.

- **PSV (Platform Supply Vessel)**: Designed to transport supplies, equipment, and personnel to offshore oil and gas platforms. PSVs typically range from 3,000-5,000 DWT with deck areas of 700-1,000 square meters. These vessels operate on time charters or spot contracts.

- **AHTS (Anchor Handling Tug Supply)**: Multi-purpose vessels combining towing, anchor handling, and supply capabilities. AHTS vessels are rated by bollard pull (typically 80-300+ tonnes) and are essential for rig moves and offshore construction.

### Commercial Shipping

- **Container vessels**: Measured in TEU capacity, ranging from feeder (500-3,000 TEU) to ultra-large (20,000+ TEU)
- **Tankers**: Classified by size (VLCC, Suezmax, Aframax, Panamax) and cargo type (crude, product, chemical, LNG)
- **Bulk carriers**: Dry cargo vessels categorized by size (Capesize, Panamax, Handymax, Handysize)
- **Ro-Ro and ferries**: Passenger and cargo ferries, car carriers, and specialized roll-on/roll-off vessels

### Specialized Vessels

- Cable laying and subsea construction vessels
- Dredgers and offshore construction vessels
- Research and survey vessels
- Crew transfer vessels (CTVs) for offshore wind

## Market Dynamics

The maritime market operates in distinct cycles driven by global trade, commodity prices, and newbuilding orderbooks.

### Supply-Demand Fundamentals

- Fleet supply is relatively inelastic in the short term due to 2-3 year newbuilding lead times
- Demand correlates with global GDP, trade volumes, and commodity prices
- Vessel utilization rates directly impact charter rates and profitability
- Scrapping decisions are influenced by regulatory requirements and market conditions

### Market Cycles

- Shipping markets are notoriously cyclical with 7-10 year super-cycles
- Offshore markets follow oil price trends with 12-18 month lag
- Understanding cycle position is critical for timing business case projections

### Current Trends

- Decarbonization driving fleet renewal and alternative fuel adoption
- Digitalization enabling predictive maintenance and voyage optimization
- Consolidation among shipowners and increased charterer concentration
- Growing offshore wind sector creating new vessel demand

## Regulatory Context

Maritime operations are governed by a complex framework of international conventions, flag state requirements, and classification society rules.

### International Maritime Organization (IMO)

The IMO is the UN agency responsible for maritime safety and environmental protection. Key regulations include:

- **SOLAS**: Safety of Life at Sea convention covering vessel construction and equipment
- **MARPOL**: Marine pollution prevention including Annex VI on air emissions
- **ISM Code**: International Safety Management requirements
- **STCW**: Standards for seafarer training and certification
- **IMO 2020**: Sulfur cap limiting fuel sulfur content to 0.5%
- **EEXI/CII**: Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index and Carbon Intensity Indicator requirements

### Classification Societies

Classification societies set technical standards and verify vessel compliance:

- **DNV**: Headquartered in Norway, largest maritime classification society globally
- **Lloyd's Register**: UK-based with strong tanker and LNG expertise
- **Bureau Veritas**: French classification society with global presence
- **ABS**: American Bureau of Shipping, strong in offshore and US-flag vessels
- **ClassNK**: Japanese classification society, leading in bulk carrier classification

### Regional Regulations

- EU ETS: Shipping included in European carbon trading from 2024
- California regulations: Additional emission requirements for vessels calling US West Coast
- Polar Code: Requirements for vessels operating in Arctic/Antarctic waters

## Value Proposition

When developing business cases for maritime solutions, emphasize these key value drivers:

### Safety Enhancement

- Reduction in incidents and near-misses
- Improved situational awareness for bridge crews
- Fatigue management and crew welfare
- Compliance with safety regulations

### Operational Efficiency

- Fuel optimization through voyage planning and trim optimization
- Reduced port turnaround times
- Improved cargo handling and stowage
- Better asset utilization and fleet scheduling

### Regulatory Compliance

- Meeting IMO 2020 sulfur requirements
- EEXI/CII compliance through operational measures
- Environmental monitoring and reporting
- Simplified audit preparation and documentation

### Fuel and Emissions Reduction

- Voyage optimization for reduced consumption
- Weather routing to avoid adverse conditions
- Hull and propeller performance monitoring
- Alternative fuel transition planning

### Uptime Maximization

- Predictive maintenance to prevent breakdowns
- Remote monitoring and diagnostics
- Spare parts optimization
- Reduced off-hire periods

### Cost Reduction

- Lower fuel costs (typically 30-50% of operating expenses)
- Reduced maintenance costs through condition-based approaches
- Optimized crewing and operational planning
- Insurance premium reductions through improved risk profile

## Go-to-Market

### Sales Channels

- **Direct sales**: For large shipowners and operators with centralized procurement
- **Distributor networks**: For fragmented markets like fishing and smaller operators
- **OEM partnerships**: Integration with newbuilding packages through shipyards
- **Classification society partnerships**: Endorsement and type approval relationships

### Key Decision Makers

- Fleet managers and technical superintendents (operational solutions)
- Technical directors and CTOs (technology investments)
- CFOs and commercial directors (business case approval)
- Ship management companies (on behalf of owners)

### Sales Cycle Considerations

- Long sales cycles (6-24 months for enterprise solutions)
- Pilot projects and proof-of-concept phases are common
- Reference vessels and case studies are critical
- Industry events (Nor-Shipping, SMM, Posidonia) are key for relationship building

### Regional Considerations

- Norway/Scandinavia: Early adopters, strong digitalization focus
- Greece: Large fleet but conservative adoption, relationship-driven
- Singapore: Hub for ship management and Asian fleets
- Germany/Netherlands: Strong in container and offshore segments

## Customer Segments

### By Ownership Structure

- **Shipowner/operators**: Own and operate vessels, full P&L responsibility
- **Charterers**: Hire vessels on time charter or voyage charter basis
- **Ship managers**: Technical and commercial management on behalf of owners
- **Fleet operators**: Operate vessels on behalf of multiple owners

### By Fleet Size

- **Large fleets** (50+ vessels): Centralized IT/OT, standardization focus, enterprise deals
- **Mid-size fleets** (10-50 vessels): Mixed decision-making, pilot-then-scale approach
- **Small operators** (1-10 vessels): Owner-operated, price-sensitive, simple solutions

### By Market Segment

- **Offshore oil & gas**: High safety requirements, major company contracts
- **Offshore wind**: Growing rapidly, focus on availability and efficiency
- **Deep-sea shipping**: Global operations, fuel efficiency priority
- **Short-sea and coastal**: Regional operations, port integration focus
- **Passenger and cruise**: Guest experience and safety focus

## Pricing

### Common Pricing Models

- **Per vessel license**: Annual or perpetual license per installed vessel
- **Subscription**: Monthly or annual SaaS model with tiered features
- **Day rate component**: Fee structure tied to vessel operating days
- **Transaction-based**: Per voyage, per port call, or per operation pricing
- **Fleet-wide enterprise**: Negotiated pricing based on total fleet size

### Pricing Benchmarks

- Basic monitoring solutions: $2,000-$10,000 per vessel per year
- Voyage optimization platforms: $10,000-$30,000 per vessel per year
- Integrated fleet management: $50,000-$200,000+ per vessel per year
- Hardware and installation: $20,000-$500,000+ per vessel (one-time)

### Value-Based Pricing Considerations

- Fuel savings potential (1-5% savings on $2-5M annual fuel spend = significant ROI)
- Risk reduction value (insurance premium impacts, incident cost avoidance)
- Regulatory compliance value (cost of non-compliance vs. solution cost)
- Uptime value (off-hire costs of $20,000-$100,000+ per day)

## Key Metrics

### Market Sizing

- Global merchant fleet: ~60,000 vessels over 1,000 GT
- Offshore support vessel fleet: ~5,000 vessels globally
- Annual newbuilding deliveries: 1,000-2,000 commercial vessels
- Total addressable market calculations should segment by vessel type and geography

### Performance Metrics

- Fuel efficiency: Consumption per nautical mile or per TEU-mile
- Utilization rate: Sailing days / available days
- EEOI: Energy Efficiency Operational Indicator (emissions per transport work)
- CII rating: A-E scale for Carbon Intensity Indicator

### Business Metrics

- ARPU: Average revenue per vessel
- Fleet penetration: Installed base / addressable fleet
- Contract duration: Average subscription or license term
- Renewal rate: Percentage of contracts renewed annually
- Implementation time: Days from contract to operational status

### Industry-Specific KPIs

- Off-hire percentage: Time vessel is unavailable vs. contracted
- Charter rate volatility: Indicator of market conditions
- Fleet age profile: Average age impacts technology adoption
- Fuel price trends: Major driver of operational priorities
