# Initiative Pitch -- Lean Business Case Template

Lightweight 1-3 page template for small internal initiatives, ideas, and quick pitches. Use when the investment is modest, the audience is internal, and a full Five Case Model would be disproportionate.

---

## When to Use This Template

- Budget request under ~500K NOK (or equivalent)
- Internal tool, process improvement, small experiment
- Audience is a direct manager or team lead, not a board or steering committee
- Decision can be made in one meeting
- Time horizon is <12 months

If the initiative grows beyond this scope, graduate to the full Five Case Model.

---

## Template

### 1. What and Why

**Problem or opportunity** (2-3 sentences):
What is broken, inefficient, or missing? Who feels the pain? What evidence do you have?

**Proposed solution** (2-3 sentences):
What do you want to do about it? Be specific enough that the reader can picture it.

**Strategic fit** (1 sentence):
Which team/department/organizational goal does this support?

**Why now** (1 sentence):
What makes this timely? Cost of waiting?

### 2. Options Considered

| Option | Description | Cost | Benefit | Risk |
|--------|------------|------|---------|------|
| Do nothing | Status quo | 0 | 0 | [cost of inaction] |
| Option A | [your proposal] | | | |
| Option B | [alternative] | | | |

Always include "do nothing." Even for a small initiative, showing you considered alternatives builds credibility.

### 3. Costs, Benefits, and Risks

**Costs:**
- One-time: [itemize]
- Recurring: [itemize]
- Total first-year cost: [sum]

**Benefits** (quantify where possible):
- Time saved: [X hours/week x Y people x hourly rate]
- Error reduction: [X% fewer incidents x cost per incident]
- Revenue impact: [if applicable]
- Qualitative: [capabilities enabled, team morale, learning]

**Key risks:**
- Risk 1: [description] -- Mitigation: [what you'll do]
- Risk 2: [description] -- Mitigation: [what you'll do]

**Simple payback:**
Total cost / monthly benefit = [X] months to break even

### 4. Recommendation and Next Steps

**Recommendation:** [Go / No-Go / Pilot first]

**Immediate next steps** (if approved):
1. [Action] -- [Who] -- [By when]
2. [Action] -- [Who] -- [By when]
3. [Action] -- [Who] -- [By when]

**Success criteria:**
How will you know this worked? [Measurable outcome] by [date].

**Check-in point:**
[Date/milestone] for progress review and go/no-go on continuation.

---

## Writing Tips for Lean Business Cases

- **Be honest about what you don't know.** "We estimate 20 hours/week savings based on [data source], but this is uncertain" is more credible than false precision.
- **Lead with the problem, not the solution.** If the reader doesn't agree the problem is worth solving, the solution doesn't matter.
- **Quantify the cost of doing nothing.** Even rough numbers ("approximately 100 hours/year spent on manual reconciliation at ~500 NOK/hour = 50K NOK/year") are better than nothing.
- **Keep it to 1-3 pages.** If you need more space, you probably need the full Five Case Model.
- **Include a pilot option.** For uncertain initiatives, "try it for 4 weeks with one team" is often more persuasive than "roll it out company-wide."
