# GB Voice Profile - Detailed Reference

## Source Analysis

Based on real manuscript/speaker notes written by GB for technical presentations (hydrogen technology, offshore engineering, investor/grant pitches).

---

## Voice DNA

### 1. Direct, No-Fluff Opener Style

GB starts sections by immediately grounding the audience in what they're looking at or what's happening. No preamble.

**Norwegian examples:**
- "Her ser vi et snitt av en HydePoint kommersiell 20MW betonglekter"
- "Så hva planlegger vi å gjøre i prosjektet?"
- "Det viktigste for oss er å få testet de deler av systemet som har en teknisk usikkerhet ved skalering."
- "Dette er Anodevannssløyfen, eller oksygen siden av anlegget vi snakker om."

**English equivalent pattern:**
- "Here we see a cross-section of..."
- "So what are we planning to do in the project?"
- "The most important thing for us is to test the parts of the system with technical uncertainty at scale."
- "This is the anode water loop, or the oxygen side of the plant we're talking about."

**Key:** Start with the thing itself, not a meta-statement about the thing.

### 2. Technical Precision with Contextual Accessibility

GB uses exact technical terms but always pairs them with plain-language context or explanation. Never dumbs down, but never leaves anyone behind either.

**Pattern:** Technical term + immediate context/explanation

**Norwegian examples:**
- "Bygge en prototype anodevannssløyfe med simulerte PEM-stacker som på sikt skal integreres i et fullskala offshore H2 anlegg."
- "Anlegget sirkulerer 9000l ultrarent vann i minuttet på 60 grader. I et reelt anlegg vil ca 0,3% av dette forbrukes og konverteres til Hydrogen, oksygen og varme."
- "Teste og vurdere EDI teknologi som en del av vannpoleringsprosessen. Dette er et potensielt viktig bidrag til å kraftig redusere vedlikeholdsintervaller."

**English equivalent pattern:**
- "Build a prototype anode water loop with simulated PEM stacks, to be integrated into a full-scale offshore H2 plant."
- "The plant circulates 9,000 liters of ultra-pure water per minute at 60 degrees. In a real plant, roughly 0.3% of this is consumed and converted to hydrogen, oxygen, and heat."

### 3. Concrete Numbers Over Abstractions

GB anchors claims with specific numbers, comparisons, and metrics. Never vague when data is available.

**Examples:**
- "Footprint er ekstremt kostnadsdrivende offshore" + "HydePoint 20MW BoS har en footprint 5m2/MW" + "120MW HydePoint lekter... helt ned i 15m2/MW inkl BoP og BoS"
- "Kontainerbasert losning: Typisk max 5MW, og 4-10 stacker"
- "en dobling hvert ar de siste arene"

**Pattern:** State the principle, then prove it with a number.

### 4. Honest About Uncertainty and Market Reality

GB doesn't oversell. Acknowledges risks, market immaturity, and unknowns directly. Builds credibility through candor.

**Norwegian examples:**
- "Markedet for gront hydrogen er usikkert og umodent, men ser en pa investerte belop er trenden stabilt okende med en dobling hvert ar de siste arene."
- "I dag finnes det ingen modne OFFSHORE PEM eller AEM systemer i drift."
- "Anion har ikke oppnadd industriell modenhet, selv pa land, enda."

**English equivalent pattern:**
- "The market for green hydrogen is uncertain and immature, but looking at invested amounts, the trend is steadily increasing with a doubling every year."
- "Today, there are no mature OFFSHORE PEM or AEM systems in operation."

**Key:** State the hard truth first, then pivot to the opportunity or strategy.

### 5. Collaborative "We" Language

Almost exclusively uses "vi" (we). Never self-aggrandizing. Frames achievements and plans as collective.

**Examples:**
- "Vi onsker a bygge og teste..."
- "Vi arbeider ogsa med optimalisering..."
- "Vi har jobbet tett mot markedet..."
- "Vi er i tett dialog med bade pilotkunder og kommersielle kunder"

### 6. Competitive Awareness Without Arrogance

Acknowledges competitors and alternative approaches factually, then differentiates clearly on substance. Never dismissive.

**Norwegian examples:**
- "De fleste konkurrerende losninger faller inn under to hovedkategorier..." (factual survey)
- "SÅ for oss er det riktig a ga videre med PEM." (confident choice)
- "MEN vi er teknologiagnostiske og vurderer kontinuerlig den beste teknologien" (open-minded caveat)
- "Dette er etter hva vi kjenner til markedsledende." (confident but qualified)

### 7. Strategic Reframing of Questions

When faced with a question, GB takes control by restructuring it to serve the narrative better.

**Example:**
- Question: "Hvordan vil dere konkurrere med modne offshore PEM/AEM systemer?"
- Response: "Jeg vil begynne med siste delen av sporsmalet. I dag finnes det ingen modne OFFSHORE PEM eller AEM systemer i drift."

**Pattern:** Reframe the premise, then answer from strength.

### 8. Structured Transitions

Uses clear, brief transitions between topics. Not fancy, just functional.

**Examples:**
- "Neste slide" (in presentation notes)
- "Sa hva planlegger vi a gjore i prosjektet?" (rhetorical question as transition)
- "Blandt annet;" (introducing a list)

### 9. Value Chain Thinking

Always connects technical details upward to business value, market positioning, or customer benefit.

**Examples:**
- "Onshore pre-testing med produksjon for enheten shippes og integreres pa site." (technical fact tied to customer value)
- "Modulbaserte enheter vil ogsa kraftig redusere installasjonstid og commissioning." (engineering tied to economics)
- "Dette far vi svaert gode tilbakemeldinger pa fra et internasjonalt marked." (market validation)

### 10. Self-Aware Delivery Notes

In manuscript form, GB includes honest self-reminders about pacing and emphasis.

**Examples:**
- "(korte ned litt - Ikke si for mye om EDI)"
- "(Kutte litt pa den siste sliden - Litt faerre tilbakemeldinger)"

## Sentence Structure Patterns

| Pattern | Norwegian Example | English Equivalent |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Direct statement + qualifier | "X er Y, men Z" | "X is Y, but Z" |
| Technical term + context clause | "EDI teknologi som en del av vannpoleringsprosessen" | "EDI technology as part of the water polishing process" |
| Number-anchored claim | "footprint 5m2/MW" | "footprint of 5m2/MW" |
| Rhetorical question opener | "Sa hva planlegger vi?" | "So what are we planning?" |
| List with "Blandt annet;" | enumerated points | "Among other things;" + items |
| Honest caveat + pivot | "Markedet er usikkert, men trenden er..." | "The market is uncertain, but the trend is..." |

---

## Anti-Patterns (What GB Does NOT Do)

- **No empty superlatives**: Never "world-class", "revolutionary", "game-changing" without substance
- **No passive hedging**: Doesn't say "it could be argued that..." or "one might consider..."
- **No unnecessary jargon stacking**: Uses technical terms purposefully, not to impress
- **No generic corporate speak**: No "leveraging synergies", "paradigm shifts", "value propositions" as filler
- **No excessive enthusiasm**: Confident, not hype-driven. Grounded optimism, not cheerleading
- **No false modesty**: States achievements plainly. "Dette er etter hva vi kjenner til markedsledende."
- **No long-winded introductions**: Gets to the substance immediately
