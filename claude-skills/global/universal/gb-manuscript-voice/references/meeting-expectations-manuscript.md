## Section: WHAT

A project starts with an unclear mandate, vague objectives, and no real definition of success, or we are hunting problems to solve with technology.

# Slide 2: The System — How It All Connects

## Section: SO WHAT

*[Display infographic: Meeting Expectations system overview]*

- It starts at the bottom: the mandate. Every project begins with a clear objective and defined measurable key results — KR1, KR2, KR3 etc.. This is where we commit to what success looks like. 
- Each project manager owns their milestones and their business case. The business case matures through the feasibility phase and stays alive throughout the following phases — This should not be a document you write once and forget.
- The data flows upward. Key result data feeds into OKR Performance and OKR Stability. Cost, estimates and dates feeds into cost and time accuracy KPIs. This is how individual project connects and aggregates to top-level dashboard.
- We have defined 5KPIs:
- Cost Accuracy is the Deviation between estimated and actual project costs
- Time Accuracy is the Deviation between planned and actual delivery dates
- OKR Performance is the Deviation between Reported Key Result Progress and expected progress
- OKR Stability is How Stable Key Results remain after initial approval, f,eks scope creep should show up here.
- Business Case Approval is the Ratio between approved business cases and total projects.
- Each of these KPIs will have target values for the division to meet.
- Together, these answer the questions leadership needs answered: are we on budget, on schedule, and delivering what was promised?
- Obviously, the dashboard is only as good as the data feeding it. So, it's increasingly important that we follow the reporting guidelines.   
We are also working on getting rid of open text fields as much as possible and replace them with dropdowns, date-pickers etc. to ease the reporting and increase the data quality.
- On the left side: Why this matters.   
Clear goals, validated business cases, measurable milestones. Work stays anchored to an approved plan. Full visibility means smarter decisions about where to invest time and money.
- On the right, What we want to avoid.   
Projects without clear goals drift - they drain energy, waste budget, and erode trust in our ability to deliver.
- A good business case tells us: who is the customer, what problems we are solving, what's the expected return, and how do we know it worked? What must be true in terms of assumptions? What's the strategic fit for the company? Etc. Ideally, the Business Case should be built upon the mandate.

# Slide 3: What This Means for You
