# House Purchase Feasibility Analysis — 11M NOK, Stavanger Area

## Summary & Recommendation

An 11M NOK house is **realistic but tight** on the numbers as stated. Your single KM income of 1.5M NOK gives a max loan of 7.5M, which means you need either **Elsa's income added to the application** or **significantly more equity than 15%**. With dual income (even if Elsa earns 500-600K), the lending cap rises to 10-10.5M and the deal becomes feasible. The smartest financing path is to use a combination of your ASK holdings and Elsa's income to qualify, while preserving as much invested capital as possible. This purchase will **delay your FI timeline by an estimated 3-7 years** depending on how much equity you liquidate and how housing appreciation plays out.

**Bottom line:** Doable with dual income, but get Elsa's exact salary nailed down before approaching the bank -- that single number determines whether you qualify.

## Analysis

### Screening -- Current Conditions

*Note: This analysis uses general knowledge and reference data. No live web search was performed. For execution, verify current rates with Norges Bank and your bank.*

- **Norges Bank styringsrente:** As of early 2026, the policy rate is in the 3.75-4.25% range. Norwegian mortgage rates are typically ~4-5% for floating rate.
- **Stavanger housing market:** Solid price development driven by the oil/energy sector and defense spending (Kongsberg). Prices for single-family homes in the 10-12M range are common in areas like Madla, Hinna, Tasta, Storhaug, and Sandnes.
- **KOG/KM stocks:** Strong performers due to defense and maritime spending. However, the entire equity position being in employer-related stocks is a **concentration risk** flag.
- **Lending regulations (Utlansforskriften):** In force. 15% equity, 5x income cap, stress test at current rate + 3 percentage points.

### Norwegian Lending Rules Applied to Your Situation

| Rule | Requirement | Your Situation |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Max loan-to-value** | 85% (15% equity required) | 15% of 11M = **1.65M equity needed** |
| **Max debt-to-income** | 5x gross household income | At 1.5M single: max 7.5M. Need **dual income** |
| **Stress test** | Afford payments at current rate + 3pp | At ~7.5% stress rate on 9.35M loan: ~65,000/month. Very tight on single income |
| **Dokumentavgift** | 2.5% of property value (selveier) | 11M * 2.5% = **275,000 NOK** |
| **Total cash needed at closing** | Equity + dokumentavgift + fees | **~1.95M NOK minimum** |

### The Critical Income Gap

With your KM income of 1.5M alone:

```
Max loan:           1,500,000 * 5 = 7,500,000 NOK
Purchase price:     11,000,000 NOK
Required equity:    11,000,000 - 7,500,000 = 3,500,000 NOK (31.8%)
You have in ASK:    ~1,500,000 NOK
Gap:                ~2,000,000 NOK
```

**You cannot finance this on single income alone.** The 5x rule is the binding constraint.

With Elsa's income added (estimates):

| Elsa's Gross Income | Combined Income | Max Loan (5x) | Equity Needed (11M - loan) | Feasible? |
|---------------------|----------------|----------------|---------------------------|-----------|
| 400,000 NOK | 1,900,000 | 9,500,000 | 1,500,000 (13.6%) | Almost -- need 15% = 1.65M |
| 500,000 NOK | 2,000,000 | 10,000,000 | 1,000,000 (9.1%) | Yes -- equity covered by ASK |
| 600,000 NOK | 2,100,000 | 10,500,000 | 500,000 (4.5%) | Yes -- comfortable |
| 700,000 NOK | 2,200,000 | 11,000,000 | 0 (but still need 15%) | Yes -- 15% rule is the floor |

**Key insight:** Even with a high combined income, you still need at minimum 15% equity = **1.65M NOK**, plus dokumentavgift of ~275K and closing costs. That totals roughly **1.95M NOK in cash/equity**. Your ASK of ~1.5M covers most of this but not all. You need an additional ~450K from savings, current home equity, or other sources.

### Scenario Modeling -- Two Financing Approaches

*Assumes Elsa earns ~600K, combined income 2.1M*


**Monthly cost:** ~72,750/month (mortgage ~52K + kommunale avgifter ~3K + forsikring ~1K + vedlikehold ~13.75K + strom ~3K)

#### Scenario B: Larger Equity, Smaller Loan (More Conservative)

*20% equity = 2.2M, loan = 8.8M*

**Monthly cost:** ~69,750/month. Stress test at ~57% of net income -- more comfortable.

**Trade-off:** Requires ~1.1M in additional savings beyond ASK, but lower monthly burden preserves savings capacity.

### Tax Implications of Selling ASK Shares

- ASK: tax only on withdrawal above total deposits. If cost basis ~1.2M, value 1.5M, tax on 300K gain = ~113,500 NOK (37.84%).
- Strategy: Only withdraw what you need. Fund part from other savings to minimize taxable withdrawal.

### Formuesskatt Consideration

- Primary home at 25% verdsetting (up to 10M). 11M home = ~3.2M tax value.
- Mortgage at 100% deduction. Net: 9.35M mortgage against 3.2M tax value = negative wealth tax impact (reduces overall formuesskatt).
- Converting ASK (80% verdsetting) to home equity is roughly neutral to slightly favorable.
